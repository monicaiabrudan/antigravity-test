# Project 2: QC Evaluation of a Novel Sequencing Platform

This project evaluates the performance of a newly acquired Illumina sequencing platform at our institute. We analyzed two Salmonella Paratyphi B isolates (**Group 2** and **Group 4**) sequenced in a single run. The reads were aligned to the complete reference genome of *Salmonella enterica* serovar Paratyphi B str. SPB7 ([CP000886.fasta](file:///Users/ma14/Project2/CP000886.fasta)).

---

## 1. Alignment Statistics (QC Evaluation)

The raw reads were aligned to the reference genome using `bwa mem` and analyzed using `samtools stats`.
* Group 2 Sorted BAM: [Group2.sorted.bam](file:///Users/ma14/Project2/Group2.sorted.bam)
* Group 4 Sorted BAM: [Group4.sorted.bam](file:///Users/ma14/Project2/Group4.sorted.bam)

Below is a comparison of the alignment QC metrics:

| Metric | Group 2 (Sample S2) | Group 4 (Sample S4) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Total Reads** | 1,019,342 | 941,442 |
| **Reads Mapped** | 942,884 (**92.5%**) | 936,751 (**99.5%**) |
| **Reads Properly Paired** | 929,774 (**91.2%**) | 931,840 (**99.0%**) |
| **Reads Unmapped** | 76,458 (**7.5%**) | 4,691 (**0.5%**) |
| **Estimated Coverage** | ~18.5x | ~19.3x |
| **Total Mismatches** | 861,448 | 92,930 |
| **Alignment Error Rate** | **0.95%** (`9.55e-3`) | **0.099%** (`9.90e-4`) |
| **Average Base Quality (Q)** | 36.2 | 36.4 |
| **Average Insert Size** | 266.7 bp (SD: 125.9) | 246.5 bp (SD: 91.3) |

### Key Observations from QC:
1. **High Unmapped Reads in Group 2:** Group 2 has a high rate of unmapped reads (7.5% vs 0.5% in Group 4).
2. **Elevated Error Rate in Group 2:** The alignment error rate in Group 2 is **10 times higher** than in Group 4 (0.95% vs 0.099%).
3. **Homopolymer Reads:** Inspection of the unmapped reads in Group 2 revealed a significant number of low-complexity, homopolymeric sequences (e.g. poly-T and poly-G runs). On Illumina 2-channel chemistry platforms, failed or dark clusters (where no signal is detected) default to calling `G` bases, leading to artificial poly-G/low-complexity reads.
4. **Insert Sizes:** Both libraries have correct and tight insert size distributions, indicating successful fragmentation and sizing.

## 2. Variant Calling & Filtering Results

We performed variant calling using `bcftools mpileup | bcftools call` and filtered the resulting VCF files using a stringent threshold of `QUAL >= 30` and `DP >= 20`.

* Group 2 Raw VCF: [Group2.vcf.gz](file:///Users/ma14/Project2/Group2.vcf.gz)
* Group 2 Filtered VCF: [Group2.filtered.vcf.gz](file:///Users/ma14/Project2/Group2.filtered.vcf.gz)
* Group 4 Raw VCF: [Group4.vcf.gz](file:///Users/ma14/Project2/Group4.vcf.gz)
* Group 4 Filtered VCF: [Group4.filtered.vcf.gz](file:///Users/ma14/Project2/Group4.filtered.vcf.gz)

Below is the comparison of variant counts before and after filtering:

| Sample | SNPs (Raw) | SNPs (Filtered) | Indels (Raw) | Indels (Filtered) | ts/tv (Raw) | ts/tv (Filtered) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Group 2** | 39,317 | **17,160** | 397 | **153** | 3.33 | **3.75** |
| **Group 4** | 105 | **17** | 24 | **6** | 1.28 | **1.12** |

### Insights:
* **Strain Divergence vs. Reference Identity:** 
  * **Group 4** has only **17 high-confidence SNPs** relative to the *Salmonella Paratyphi B* str. SPB7 reference genome. This isolate is virtually identical to the reference strain.
  * **Group 2** has **17,160 high-confidence SNPs** even after strict filtering. This indicates that Group 2 is a genetically distinct strain of *Salmonella Paratyphi B* that is divergent from the SPB7 reference.
* **Transition/Transversion (ts/tv) Ratio:**
  * Group 2's filtered callset has a ts/tv ratio of **3.75**. In bacteria, a high ts/tv ratio (> 3.0) indicates real biological variants, particularly transitions driven by selective pressure or CpG biases.
  * The high ts/tv ratio confirms that the 17,160 SNPs called in Group 2 are **real biological variations** and not sequencing errors.

## 3. Conclusions and Recommendations for the Head of the Institute

We can draw the following conclusions and formulate recommendations for the head of the institute:

### 1. Was the sequencing run a success?
**Yes, it was a success.** 
* Group 4 produced exceptionally clean data (99.5% mapping rate, 99.0% properly paired reads, and a very low error rate of 0.099%). 
* While Group 2 had higher unmapped reads (7.5%) and a higher raw error rate (0.95%), it still successfully generated enough coverage (~18.5x) to call 17,160 high-confidence SNPs with a highly biological ts/tv ratio of 3.75.

### 2. Should the institute keep the sequencing instrument?
**Yes, absolutely.**
* The Group 4 metrics demonstrate that the sequencing instrument is fully capable of producing high-throughput, extremely high-quality NGS data (error rate < 0.1% and mapping rate > 99%). The instrument itself is functional and meets Illumina's standard specifications.

### 3. What caused the issues in Group 2?
The lower performance of Group 2 is likely due to **library preparation issues** or **sample-specific contamination**, rather than a hardware defect of the sequencer:
* The presence of low-complexity, homopolymeric unmapped reads suggests a library concentration issue (low input DNA) or adapter-dimer contamination, which causes clusters to drop signal early, leading to poly-G calls on 2-channel chemistry machines.
* If the two samples were run in different lanes, a lane-specific fluidics clog or bubble could also explain the elevated error rate in that lane.

### 4. Recommendations Moving Forward
1. **Strengthen Library QC:** Implement strict quality control steps (e.g., library concentration and size checking via Bioanalyzer/TapeStation) before loading libraries onto the flowcell.
2. **Optimize Cluster Density:** Ensure proper loading concentrations to avoid under-clustering or over-clustering.
3. **Review Run Reports:** Inspect the instrument's run reports (SAV / interop files) to compare Lane 1 vs. Lane 2 metrics (e.g., %PF clusters, phasing/prephasing) to rule out lane-specific hardware issues.
4. **Pre-processing/Trimming:** Implement a standard pre-processing trimming step (using `fastp` or `trimmomatic`) in our analysis pipeline. Trimming adapters and low-quality bases before alignment will reduce the unmapped reads and error rate for libraries like Group 2.
